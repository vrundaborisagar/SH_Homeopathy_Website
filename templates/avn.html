AVN (Avascular Necrosis)

Avascular Necrosis (AVN) is a condition where the blood supply to a bone is disrupted or reduced, leading to the gradual death of bone tissue. Without enough blood, the bone weakens, can collapse, and often causes serious pain and stiffness, especially in joints like the hip.

How to Help Prevent It:

  • Avoid long-term use of steroids without supervision
  • Limit excessive alcohol consumption
  • Maintain a healthy lifestyle with regular movement
  • Treat joint injuries promptly
  • Keep chronic conditions like diabetes and high cholesterol under control

Stages of AVN:

Stage I
• Bone appears mostly normal on X-rays, but blood supply is reduced.
• Mild pain or stiffness may be present. Early treatment can slow progression.

Stage II
• Small cracks appear as blood supply worsens.
• Femoral head becomes rougher. Pain and stiffness increase.

Stage III
• Collapse or flattening of the femoral head begins.
• Movement becomes difficult, pain worsens, visible collapse on X-ray.

Stage IV
• Severe joint damage and arthritis develop.
• Constant pain, surgery may be needed for relief.

Symptoms:

  • Mild to severe joint pain that worsens with activity
  • Stiffness or limited joint movement
  • Difficulty putting weight on the joint (hip, knee, etc.)
  • Pain at rest or during sleep
  • Limping or change in walking style

What Makes It Worse:

  • Ignoring early signs and delaying treatment
  • Continued weight-bearing and high activity
  • Alcohol abuse and smoking
  • Uncontrolled health issues like cholesterol or diabetes
  • Repeat trauma to the joint
