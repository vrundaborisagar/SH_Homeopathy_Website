Sciatica

Sciatica refers to pain that radiates along the path of the sciatic nerve, which branches from the lower back through the hips and buttocks and down each leg. It typically affects only one side of the body. The condition is commonly caused by herniated discs, bone spurs, or spinal narrowing compressing part of the nerve. Sciatica can cause intense discomfort, weakness, or numbness along the nerve path.

Symptoms

  • Sharp or burning pain in the lower back, buttock, or leg
  • Numbness or tingling sensation in the leg or foot
  • Weakness in the affected leg
  • Pain that worsens while sitting, standing up, or walking
  • Difficulty bending, twisting, or lifting objects
  • Discomfort on one side of the body, often radiating below the knee

What Makes It Worse

  • Prolonged sitting or standing
  • Lifting heavy objects without proper posture
  • Sudden twisting of the spine
  • Being overweight or inactive
  • Stress and tension in the lower back muscles
  • Neglecting early signs of back pain

Precautions

  • Use proper posture while sitting or lifting
  • Practice gentle stretching and core-strengthening exercises
  • Avoid prolonged sitting without movement
  • Maintain a healthy weight
  • Use ergonomic chairs and sleep on supportive mattresses
  • Consult your doctor early if pain radiates down the leg
