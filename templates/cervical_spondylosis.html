Cervical Spondylosis

Cervical spondylosis is a degenerative condition that affects the bones, discs, and joints of the neck (cervical spine). It is commonly caused by age-related wear and tear that results in thinning of the spinal discs and development of bone spurs. This can lead to stiffness, pain, and restricted neck movement. Cervical spondylosis is very common after middle age, and its severity varies from mild discomfort to serious nerve compression.

Symptoms

  • Persistent pain and stiffness in the neck
  • Pain radiating to shoulders, arms, or hands
  • Numbness or tingling sensation in arms or fingers
  • Muscle weakness in the arms or legs
  • Headaches, especially at the back of the head
  • Difficulty moving the neck or turning the head
  • Feeling dizzy or having balance issues in severe cases

Precautions

  • Maintain a good posture while sitting, working, or using a mobile phone
  • Avoid holding your neck in one position for too long
  • Sleep on a comfortable pillow that supports your neck properly
  • Do gentle neck exercises as advised by your doctor
  • Avoid sudden, jerky movements of the neck
  • Limit lifting heavy objects
  • Take regular breaks during long hours at a computer or desk
