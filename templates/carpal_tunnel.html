Carpal Tunnel Syndrome

Carpal Tunnel Syndrome is a painful condition caused by pressure on the median nerve as it passes through the narrow carpal tunnel in the wrist. It often leads to numbness, tingling, and weakness in the hand and fingers, especially at night or after repetitive movements. Early care and proper management can help reduce symptoms and prevent long-term damage.

Symptoms

  • Numbness or tingling in the thumb, index, and middle fingers
  • Burning sensation or pain that may radiate up the arm
  • Weak grip and difficulty holding small objects
  • Symptoms often worsen at night or with repetitive tasks
  • Feeling of swelling in fingers without visible swelling

Carpal Tunnel Syndrome is a common condition that develops when the median nerve, which runs through a small passage in the wrist called the carpal tunnel, becomes compressed or irritated. This can cause pain, tingling, and numbness in the fingers and hand, making everyday tasks like typing, gripping, or lifting difficult. Early attention and gentle, supportive treatments can help ease discomfort and prevent the condition from getting worse.

What Makes It Worse

  • Repetitive wrist movements like typing or using tools
  • Poor wrist posture for long periods
  • Excessive pressure on the wrist joint
  • Conditions like diabetes, thyroid issues, or pregnancy
  • Ignoring early symptoms and delaying proper care

What Can Help Prevent Carpal Tunnel Syndrome

  • Maintain a neutral wrist position while working or using tools
  • Take frequent breaks from repetitive hand movements
  • Do gentle wrist stretches and exercises regularly
  • Use an ergonomic setup at your desk and keyboard
  • Avoid putting too much pressure directly on the wrist
  • Treat related conditions like diabetes or arthritis promptly
