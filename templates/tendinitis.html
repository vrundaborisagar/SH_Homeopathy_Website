Tendinitis

Tendinitis is the inflammation or irritation of a tendon — the thick fibrous cords that attach muscle to bone. It causes pain and tenderness just outside a joint, most commonly in the shoulders, elbows, wrists, knees, or heels. Overuse, injury, or aging can all lead to this condition. Left untreated, it may result in tendon rupture or chronic joint pain, significantly affecting mobility and daily life.

Symptoms

  • Localized pain or tenderness near a joint
  • Swelling and stiffness around the tendon area
  • Increased pain during movement or activity
  • Warmth or redness in the affected area
  • Difficulty performing tasks that involve the joint

What Makes It Worse

  • Repetitive strain or overuse of the affected joint
  • Sudden, heavy exertion or injury
  • Poor posture and improper joint mechanics
  • Ignoring pain and continuing stressful activities
  • Lack of rest and recovery time

Precautions

  • Warm up and stretch properly before physical activity
  • Avoid overuse of affected joints
  • Maintain good posture and ergonomic practices
  • Apply cold packs to reduce inflammation
  • Follow a guided physical therapy routine
