O.A. Knee with Gout

O.A. Knee with Gout is a painful condition where long-term wear and tear of the knee joint is combined with sudden attacks of uric acid crystals. The damaged cartilage from osteoarthritis and excess uric acid cause inflammation, swelling, and stiffness. This leads to difficulty in moving the knee and increased pain, especially during flare-ups.

Symptoms:

  • Sharp, intense pain in the knee
  • Swelling, warmth, and redness around the joint
  • Stiffness and reduced range of motion
  • Increased discomfort with movement or even at rest
  • Frequent flare-ups that worsen the pain

What Makes It Worse:

  • Eating foods high in purines (red meat, seafood, alcohol)
  • Excess weight and lack of regular movement
  • Dehydration and insufficient water intake
  • Excessive stress or over-straining the knee joint
  • Ignoring early signs and delaying treatment
