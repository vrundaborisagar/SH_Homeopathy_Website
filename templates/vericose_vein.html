Varicose Veins

Varicose veins are enlarged, swollen, and twisted veins that typically appear on the legs. They are often blue or dark purple and may bulge from under the skin. Caused by faulty valves in the veins that allow blood to pool, varicose veins can lead to aching, heaviness, and discomfort — especially after long hours of standing or sitting. While common, untreated cases may result in complications such as ulcers, clots, or skin changes.

Stages of Vein Disease

Vein diseases progress through five stages — from spider veins to venous ulcers. Each stage represents worsening vein health, with increasing discomfort, swelling, and risk of complications. Early signs include minor cosmetic changes, but without timely care, the condition may escalate to painful ulcers. Dr. Borisagar’s SH Homeopathy Kit supports recovery at each stage, targeting root causes, easing symptoms, and helping patients avoid surgery through natural healing and vein strengthening remedies.

Stage 1 - Spider Veins: Small, thin veins visible on the surface of the skin.

Stage 2 - Varicose Veins: Enlarged, twisted veins with discomfort or heaviness.

Stage 3 - Leg Swelling: Swelling in ankles and lower legs, especially by the end of the day.

Stage 4 - Skin Discoloration: Darkening and changes in skin texture around the veins.

Stage 5 - Venous Ulcers: Open sores or ulcers that take time to heal and may get infected.

Symptoms

  • Bulging, twisted veins visible under the skin
  • Heaviness, aching, or burning sensation in the legs
  • Swelling of ankles and feet by evening
  • Itching or discoloration around the veins
  • Leg cramps or throbbing at night
  • Skin changes or ulcer formation in severe cases

What Makes It Worse

  • Standing or sitting for long periods
  • Lack of physical activity
  • Obesity or excess weight
  • Tight clothing that restricts blood flow
  • High heels worn for long hours
  • Ignoring initial vein bulging and swelling

Precautions

  • Keep legs elevated when resting
  • Wear compression stockings if advised
  • Stay active with regular walking and leg movements
  • Avoid sitting or standing too long in one position
  • Maintain a healthy weight
  • Use loose, comfortable clothing to improve circulation
