Spinal Cord Injury

A spinal cord injury (SCI) is damage to any part of the spinal cord or nerves at the end of the spinal canal. This can lead to permanent changes in strength, sensation, and body functions below the site of the injury. It may be caused by trauma such as a fall, car accident, sports injury, or medical conditions like infections or tumors. Early treatment and specialized care are crucial to recovery.

Symptoms

  • Loss of movement or paralysis in any part of the body
  • Altered sensation, including the ability to feel heat, cold, or touch
  • Loss of bowel or bladder control
  • Spasms or exaggerated reflexes
  • Pain or intense stinging sensation due to nerve damage
  • Difficulty breathing or coughing (for upper spinal injuries)

What Makes It Worse

  • Delayed or improper medical care after injury
  • Overexertion without physical therapy guidance
  • Infections due to poor hygiene or bed sores
  • Depression, anxiety, or lack of mental support
  • Inconsistent or inappropriate home care routines
  • Ignoring early signs of nerve dysfunction

Precautions

  • Follow physiotherapy and rehab exercises strictly
  • Use pressure-relieving mattresses to prevent sores
  • Practice bladder and bowel training
  • Monitor skin regularly for wounds or infections
  • Ensure proper nutrition and hydration
  • Get emotional and psychological support
